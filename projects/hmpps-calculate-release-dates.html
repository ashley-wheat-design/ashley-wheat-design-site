Improving calculations in prisons

The Calculate release dates service replaces manual, paper-based release date calculations in prisons, reducing the risk of error and saving staff time.

The Review calculation page showing the release dates calculated by the service, including a breakdown of how they were reached.

Review calculation page

Calculate release dates is used by offender management unit staff in prisons to calculate prisoners’ release dates based on court case and sentencing information.

Prior to the service, release date calculations were carried out manually using paper-based processes. Staff were required to interpret complex sentencing information, apply sentencing rules, and carry out multiple checks by hand, often across different systems and documents.

Given the complexity of sentencing rules and the reliance on paper-based processes in a high-pressure environment, there was a risk of errors in release date calculations, which could lead to prisoners being released in error.

I led the interaction design for the Calculate release dates, designing the end-to-end service used by staff to review sentence and offence information and automatically calculate accurate release dates.

What I did

Working closely with user researchers, content designers, developers and subject matter experts, I led the interaction design for the service, shaping how complex sentencing information is reviewed and how automated release date calculations are presented.

  • Designed the core user journeys and interactions for reviewing sentence and offence information and calculating release dates, using the GOV.UK and MoJ Design Systems
  • Shaped the service around transparency and trust, making it clear how sentence information contributes to the final calculated release dates
  • Designed detailed calculation breakdowns to help users understand, check and verify how outcomes had been reached
  • Worked with the service designer, user researchers and content designers to run a design sprint exploring problems with court case and sentence information held in a legacy system
  • Helped identify that Calculate release dates relied on accurate court case data entered upstream, shaping the strategy for a wider programme of services including Remand and sentencing, Adjustments and Recalls
  • Ran workshops with stakeholders and subject matter experts to redesign release date notification slips given to prisoners, focusing on using plain language and helping prisoners with accessibility needs

Outcome

Calculate release dates is an award-winning service and has been recognised as one of the CDDO Top 75 digital services across government.

As of May 2022, Calculate release dates had supported over 79,000 release date recalculations, saving an estimated 761 days of staff time and helping prevent 92 potential releases in error.

The service was rolled out at scale earlier than expected across the prison estate, becoming a critical tool supporting early release schemes introduced as part of SDS40 and SDS50.
