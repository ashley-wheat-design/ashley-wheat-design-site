Making it easier to find information on NHS.UK

Improving the information architecture of NHS.UK to make it easier for people to find health and service information.

Findings from usability testing of the pregnancy section of the NHS website.

NHS.UK provides trusted health information and guidance to millions of people across the UK and worldwide. As the site grew over time, information became more difficult to find and navigation more complex. Users struggled to find health and service information quickly and easily, sometimes when they needed it most.

This work focused on improving the underlying information architecture of the NHS website, understanding how users expect to find information and how their mental models shape navigation. This enabled the design of new ways of helping all users, including those with accessibility needs, to navigate NHS.UK, find health information, access services and complete transactional journeys.

What I did

I led user research as part of the NHS.UK design team, focusing on how people find and navigate health and service information.

  • Led regular usability research to explore navigation, findability and content structure across NHS.UK, based on users’ top tasks
  • Worked closely within the design team to iterate navigational components, taxonomy and labelling based on research findings from usability studies, card sorting and tree testing
  • Collaborated with an external consultancy to develop an information architecture toolkit to support ongoing improvements to NHS.UK
  • Carried out usability research focused on the pregnancy section of the NHS website, testing navigational components and patterns in depth

Outcome

The information architecture work informed ongoing improvements to NHS.UK, helping people find and navigate health and service information more easily.

The navigational components and patterns developed through this work supported further IA activity across NHS.UK, providing teams with a clearer, research-led way to evolve navigation and content structure.

This contributed to a more consistent and accessible approach to organising information and services on NHS.UK, grounded in user needs.
