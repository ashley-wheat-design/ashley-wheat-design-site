Redesigning the NHS website

Redesigning NHS.UK to make health information clearer, more accessible and easier to use for millions of people.

The NHS.UK homepage.

NHS.UK home page

NHS.UK is the public-facing home of the NHS online. It provides trusted health information, guidance and services to millions of people across the UK.

NHS Choices, as it was previously known, had grown over time and contained a large volume of content delivered through inconsistent templates and patterns. This made it harder for users to find and understand information, and created challenges in maintaining accessibility and consistency at scale.

Improving NHS.UK meant balancing the needs of members of the public across the UK, including people with access needs or low digital literacy, with the requirement to convey accurate and clinically accurate medical information, often at times when people may be anxious or vulnerable.

I led user research on the NHS.UK redesign, working closely with interaction designers, content designers, developers and stakeholders to ensure design decisions were grounded in evidence from real users.

What I did

Working as the lead user researcher on the NHS.UK redesign, I focused on testing and validating designs, ensuring decisions were grounded in evidence from real users.

  • Tested components, page templates and end-to-end user journeys with users, identifying usability and accessibility issues
  • Planned and facilitated multi-method user research including in-person usability testing, pop-up research, surveys and click tests to gather both qualitative and quantitative insights
  • Ran a design workshop with designers and other stakeholders to support the redesign of the NHS.UK cookie banner and controls, balancing user needs with requirements under GDPR and PECR
  • Worked closely with interaction designers and content designers to make clear, evidence-based design recommendations and iterate components based on user research findings
  • Helped build the business case for reviewing and improving the NHS.UK information architecture, gathering supporting evidence tree testing and user research
  • Worked with the product owner to help lay the foundations for the NHS Accessibility Lab, supporting longer-term capability around inclusive research and accessibility testing

Accessibility

Accessibility was a core focus of the NHS.UK redesign.

Leading user research throughout the redesign, I made sure users with access needs were included and represented at all stages. Over half of research participants had access needs, allowing the team to focus design decisions on users with the most complex needs, so the service would also work well for everyone else.

Outcome

The redesigned NHS website was rolled out on NHS.UK and established a new, user-focused approach to presenting health information and information about NHS services in an accessible way.

The work on the redesign went on to form the basis of the NHS Design System and the NHS Service Manual, with components and patterns reused across NHS services.

This has supported NHS.UK in remaining a trusted source of health information, with the site continually iterated and improved over time.
