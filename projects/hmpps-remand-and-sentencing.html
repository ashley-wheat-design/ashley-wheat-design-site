Helping prisons capture accurate court case and sentence information

The Remand and sentencing service is used by staff in offender management units to record and manage complex court case and sentencing information.

The Court cases page showing the most recent court case.

Court cases page

Within prisons, offender management units are responsible for capturing and entering court case, offence and sentencing information from warrants issued by the courts. This information is used to calculate prisoners’ release dates. Accuracy is critical, as incorrectly captured information can lead to miscalculated release dates and, in some cases, releases in error.

I led the interaction design for Remand and sentencing, a new service replacing a legacy system used by offender management units to record legally complex court case and sentencing information. The service supports users to enter and manage court case and sentencing information more easily and accurately.

What I did

Working closely with user researchers, content designers, developers and subject matter experts, I led the interaction design for the service, shaping how complex court case and sentencing information is captured, validated and presented.

  • Designed end-to-end user journeys and interactions for capturing court case and sentencing information using the GOV.UK and MoJ Design Systems
  • Built a fully working prototype in HTML and JavaScript using the GOV.UK Prototype Kit
  • Worked closely with user research to test and iterate the designs with offender management unit staff
  • Worked with the service designer to run workshops with stakeholders and subject matter experts to explore and agree approaches to complex problems
  • Created a design manual of reusable components and patterns to support the consistent use of court case, sentence and offence information across HMPPS services

Outcome

The service was tested with offender management unit staff throughout alpha and performed well. This gave the team and stakeholders within HMPPS confidence in the design.

The work progressed to a private beta, with the designs and prototype used to support onward delivery.

Design components and patterns developed as part of the service are being used across Ministry of Justice services, supporting consistency in how court case and sentence information is presented.
